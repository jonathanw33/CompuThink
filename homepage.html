What is Computational Thinking?

Computational Thinking is a fundamental problem-solving process that involves approaching challenges systematically. It helps break down complex problems into manageable steps, using principles like abstraction, decomposition, pattern recognition, and algorithm design.

Abstraction

Discover the essence of simplification.

Decomposition

Break down complex problems into manageable parts.

Pattern Recognition

Identify trends and similarities in data.

Algorithm Design

Create step-by-step solutions to problems.

What are Virtual Labs?

Virtual Labs are interactive online environments that simulate real-world scenarios, allowing users to experiment, learn, and practice skills in a controlled setting. They provide a hands-on approach to learning, enabling users to engage with complex concepts through practical application.
